# Chromophore Guide

Understanding the chemical causes of colour in gemstones.

## Colour Mechanisms

### 1. Idiochromatic Colouring
Colour-causing element is essential to the mineral's chemical formula.

| Gemstone | Essential Element | Colour |
|----------|-------------------|--------|
| Peridot | Fe²⁺ | Green-yellow |
| Almandine | Fe²⁺ | Red-brown |
| Spessartine | Mn²⁺ | Orange |
| Malachite | Cu²⁺ | Green |
| Turquoise | Cu²⁺ | Blue-green |
| Lapis lazuli | S₃⁻ (sulfur) | Blue |
| Rhodochrosite | Mn²⁺ | Pink |
| Rhodonite | Mn²⁺ | Pink |

### 2. Allochromatic Colouring
Colour caused by trace impurities not essential to the formula.

| Gemstone | Trace Element | Colour | Concentration |
|----------|---------------|--------|---------------|
| Ruby | Cr³⁺ | Red | ~1-3% Cr₂O₃ |
| Emerald | Cr³⁺ and/or V³⁺ | Green | ~0.1-0.5% |
| Blue sapphire | Fe²⁺/Ti⁴⁺ | Blue | ~0.5-1% |
| Alexandrite | Cr³⁺ | Red/green | ~0.4% Cr₂O₃ |
| Tsavorite | V³⁺ and/or Cr³⁺ | Green | Variable |
| Tanzanite | V³⁺ | Blue-violet | ~0.02% V₂O₃ |

### 3. Colour Centres
Defects in crystal structure that absorb light.

| Gemstone | Centre Type | Colour | Notes |
|----------|-------------|--------|-------|
| Smoky quartz | Al substitution + radiation | Brown-grey | Electron hole |
| Amethyst | Fe⁴⁺ + radiation | Purple | Electron hole |
| Blue topaz (treated) | Radiation-induced | Blue | Unstable naturally |
| Maxixe beryl | NO₃⁻ + radiation | Deep blue | Fades in light |
| Yellow sapphire | Colour centre | Yellow | Some types |

### 4. Charge Transfer
Electron movement between adjacent ions.

| Gemstone | Transfer Type | Colour |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| Blue sapphire | Fe²⁺ → Ti⁴⁺ | Blue |
| Kyanite | Fe²⁺ → Fe³⁺ | Blue |
| Iolite | Fe²⁺ → Fe³⁺ | Blue-violet |
| Some aquamarine | Fe²⁺ → Fe³⁺ | Blue |

### 5. Physical Phenomena
Colour from physical structures, not chemistry.

| Effect | Cause | Examples |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Play-of-colour | Diffraction | Opal |
| Labradorescence | Interference | Labradorite |
| Adularescence | Scattering | Moonstone |
| Iridescence | Thin films | Pearl, ammolite |

## Major Chromophore Ions

### Chromium (Cr³⁺)

**Produces**: Red, pink, or green depending on crystal field

| Host Mineral | Colour | Crystal Field |
|--------------|--------|---------------|
| Corundum (ruby) | Red | Strong |
| Beryl (emerald) | Green | Moderate |
| Chrysoberyl (alexandrite) | Red ↔ Green | Intermediate |
| Spinel | Red-pink | Moderate |
| Grossular (tsavorite) | Green | Moderate |
| Tourmaline | Green | Variable |
| Jadeite | Green | Variable |

**Diagnostic features**:
- Red fluorescence under UV (in low-iron hosts)
- Characteristic absorption at 694nm (ruby) or 683/680nm (emerald)
- Colour-change possible in alexandrite

### Vanadium (V³⁺)

**Produces**: Green, blue-green, or colour-change

| Host Mineral | Colour |
|--------------|--------|
| Grossular (tsavorite) | Green (often with Cr) |
| Zoisite (tanzanite) | Blue-violet |
| Beryl | Green (some emeralds) |
| Chrysoberyl | Green-yellow |
| Kornerupine | Green |
| Colour-change garnet | Red ↔ Green |

**Diagnostic features**:
- Can produce alexandrite-like colour change
- Often combined with chromium
- Weaker fluorescence than Cr alone

### Iron (Fe²⁺ and Fe³⁺)

**Fe²⁺ (Ferrous) produces**: Blue, green, yellow-green

| Host Mineral | Colour |
|--------------|--------|
| Aquamarine | Blue-green |
| Peridot | Green (idiochromatic) |
| Spinel (some) | Blue |
| Tourmaline (indicolite) | Blue |
| Almandine | Red (idiochromatic) |

**Fe³⁺ (Ferric) produces**: Yellow, brown

| Host Mineral | Colour |
|--------------|--------|
| Yellow sapphire | Yellow |
| Chrysoberyl | Yellow |
| Citrine | Yellow |
| Heliodor | Yellow |

**Fe²⁺/Ti⁴⁺ charge transfer**: Deep blue (sapphire)

**Diagnostic features**:
- Quenches fluorescence
- Creates characteristic absorption bands
- May cause magnetic attraction in high concentrations

### Manganese (Mn²⁺ and Mn³⁺)

**Mn²⁺ produces**: Pink, orange

| Host Mineral | Colour |
|--------------|--------|
| Morganite | Pink |
| Spessartine | Orange (idiochromatic) |
| Rhodochrosite | Pink (idiochromatic) |
| Rhodonite | Pink (idiochromatic) |

**Mn³⁺ produces**: Red, pink

| Host Mineral | Colour |
|--------------|--------|
| Red beryl | Red |
| Kunzite | Pink-violet |
| Rubellite tourmaline | Pink-red |

**Diagnostic features**:
- Can produce fluorescence
- Multiple absorption bands in visible spectrum

### Copper (Cu²⁺)

**Produces**: Blue, green, turquoise

| Material | Colour |
|----------|--------|
| Turquoise | Blue-green (idiochromatic) |
| Malachite | Green (idiochromatic) |
| Paraíba tourmaline | Neon blue-green |
| Chrysocolla | Blue-green |
| Azurite | Blue |

**Diagnostic features**:
- Distinctive neon quality in Paraíba tourmaline
- Blue-green colour range characteristic
- No typical fluorescence

### Cobalt (Co²⁺)

**Produces**: Intense blue

| Material | Colour | Natural/Synthetic |
|----------|--------|-------------------|
| Blue spinel (synthetic) | Blue | Synthetic |
| Cobaltocalcite | Pink | Natural (rare) |
| Some natural spinels | Blue | Natural (very rare) |

**Diagnostic features**:
- Very strong blue colour
- Triple absorption band (635, 580, 540nm)
- Strong red fluorescence in synthetics

### Titanium (Ti³⁺ and Ti⁴⁺)

**Ti⁴⁺ involvement**: Usually in charge transfer

| Host Mineral | Role |
|--------------|------|
| Blue sapphire | Fe²⁺/Ti⁴⁺ charge transfer |
| Star corundum | TiO₂ (rutile) silk |
| Some rose quartz | Ti⁴⁺ + other elements |

**Ti³⁺ produces**: Pink-violet (rare)

| Host Mineral | Colour |
|--------------|--------|
| Some pink sapphires | Pink (rare) |

## Colour and Crystal Field Theory

The same ion can produce different colours depending on the host crystal structure:

### Chromium in Different Hosts

| Host | Coordination | Crystal Field | Colour |
|------|--------------|---------------|--------|
| Corundum | Octahedral (distorted) | Strong | Red |
| Beryl | Octahedral (distorted) | Moderate | Green |
| Chrysoberyl | Octahedral | Intermediate | Red ↔ Green |
| Spinel | Octahedral | Moderate | Red-pink |

### Iron in Different Hosts

| Host | Oxidation State | Environment | Colour |
|------|-----------------|-------------|--------|
| Aquamarine | Fe²⁺ | Channel sites | Blue-green |
| Sapphire (blue) | Fe²⁺/Ti⁴⁺ | Charge transfer | Blue |
| Sapphire (yellow) | Fe³⁺ | Substitutional | Yellow |
| Peridot | Fe²⁺ | Idiochromatic | Green |

## Treatment and Colour

### Heat Treatment Effects

| Original | Heated | Chemical Change |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| Brownish zircon | Blue | Reduction of Fe³⁺ to Fe²⁺ |
| Pale aquamarine | Deeper blue | Fe³⁺ → Fe²⁺ |
| Amethyst | Citrine | Colour centre modification |
| Purple sapphire | Pink | Colour centre removal |
| Geuda sapphire | Blue | Fe²⁺/Ti⁴⁺ activation |

### Irradiation Effects

| Original | Irradiated | Change |
|----------|------------|--------|
| Colourless topaz | Blue | Colour centre creation |
| Colourless quartz | Smoky | Al-related centres |
| Yellow beryl | Blue (Maxixe) | NO₃⁻ centres |
| Pale tourmaline | Intense pink | Mn enhancement |

## Quick Reference: Colour by Cause

| Colour | Common Chromophores |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Red** | Cr³⁺ (ruby, spinel), Mn³⁺ (red beryl), Fe³⁺ + Mn (garnet) |
| **Pink** | Cr³⁺ (low), Mn²⁺ (morganite), Mn³⁺ (kunzite) |
| **Orange** | Mn²⁺ (spessartine), Fe³⁺ + Cr³⁺ (sapphire) |
| **Yellow** | Fe³⁺ (heliodor, sapphire, chrysoberyl), colour centres |
| **Green** | Cr³⁺ (emerald), V³⁺ (tsavorite), Fe²⁺ (peridot), Cu²⁺ (turquoise) |
| **Blue** | Fe²⁺/Ti⁴⁺ (sapphire), Cu²⁺ (Paraíba), Fe²⁺ (aquamarine) |
| **Violet** | V³⁺ (tanzanite), Mn³⁺ + Fe (amethyst), Cr³⁺ + Fe (sapphire) |
| **Brown** | Fe³⁺, colour centres (smoky quartz) |

## Detection Notes

1. **Spectroscopy** reveals chromophore absorption patterns
2. **Fluorescence** behaviour indicates iron content (Fe quenches)
3. **Colour distribution** can indicate natural vs. treated
4. **Pleochroism** intensity relates to chromophore concentration
5. **Colour change** materials have specific absorption patterns
