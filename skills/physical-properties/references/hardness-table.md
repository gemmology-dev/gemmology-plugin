## Hardness Groups for Jewellery Use

### Excellent for Daily Ring Wear (H ≥ 8)
- Corundum (ruby, sapphire) - 9
- Chrysoberyl (alexandrite, cat's eye) - 8.5
- Topaz - 8
- Spinel - 8

### Good for Ring Wear (H 7-7.5)
- Beryl (emerald, aquamarine) - 7.5-8
- Tourmaline - 7-7.5
- Garnet - 7-7.5
- Quartz varieties - 7
- Zircon - 7

### Caution for Ring Wear (H 6-7)
- Tanzanite - 6-7 (plus cleavage risk)
- Peridot - 6.5-7
- Spodumene (kunzite) - 6.5-7 (plus cleavage)
- Jadeite - 6.5-7 (but very tough)

### Not Recommended for Rings (H < 6)
- Opal - 5.5-6.5
- Apatite - 5
- Sphene - 5-5.5
- Fluorite - 4
- Rhodochrosite - 3.5-4.5

## Testing Notes

- **Never scratch-test valuable stones**
- Use pencil hardness testers on non-gem surfaces if needed
- Hardness can help separate look-alikes (ruby H9 vs garnet H7)
- Remember: Hardness ≠ Durability (toughness and cleavage matter too)
