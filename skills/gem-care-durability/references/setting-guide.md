# Gemstone Setting Guide

Optimal setting styles for gemstone protection and display.

## Setting Types Overview

### Prong Settings

**Description**: Metal claws hold stone in place

| Prong Count | Best For | Protection Level |
|-------------|----------|------------------|
| 4 prong | Durable gems | Moderate |
| 6 prong | Valuable gems | Good |
| 8+ prong | Large gems | Very good |

**Advantages**:
- Maximum light entry
- Easy cleaning
- Classic appearance

**Disadvantages**:
- Corners exposed (4 prong)
- Prongs can catch/bend
- Regular maintenance needed

**Best for**: Ruby, sapphire, spinel, quartz, topaz

### Bezel Settings

**Description**: Metal rim completely surrounds girdle

| Type | Protection | Best For |
|------|------------|----------|
| Full bezel | Maximum | Fragile gems |
| Partial bezel | Good | Balance of style/protection |
| Tube bezel | Very good | Round stones |

**Advantages**:
- Excellent protection
- Secure hold
- Modern/sleek look
- Protects girdle

**Disadvantages**:
- Less light entry
- Harder to clean
- Stone appears smaller

**Best for**: Emerald, tanzanite, opal, moonstone, kunzite

### Channel Settings

**Description**: Stones set between two metal walls

**Advantages**:
- Good protection
- Sleek appearance
- Secure hold

**Disadvantages**:
- Difficult to clean
- Must match sizes
- Limited light

**Best for**: Calibrated sapphires, rubies, durable gems

### Pave Settings

**Description**: Small stones set close together, held by small beads

**Advantages**:
- Maximum sparkle
- Surface coverage
- Elegant appearance

**Disadvantages**:
- Stones can loosen
- Difficult to repair
- High maintenance

**Best for**: Small diamonds, durable small gems

### Tension Settings

**Description**: Stone held by pressure of metal band

**Advantages**:
- Modern appearance
- Stone appears to float

**Disadvantages**:
- Limited to durable gems
- Sizing difficult
- Less secure

**Best for**: Very hard gems only (sapphire, ruby)

**NOT for**: Softer or cleaving gems

### Flush/Gypsy Settings

**Description**: Stone set into metal surface, flush with top

**Advantages**:
- Maximum protection
- Very secure
- Low profile

**Disadvantages**:
- Less brilliance
- Difficult cleaning
- Stone hard to see

**Best for**: Active lifestyles, durable gems

### Halo Settings

**Description**: Centre stone surrounded by smaller stones

**Advantages**:
- Centre appears larger
- Added protection
- Extra sparkle

**Disadvantages**:
- More complex maintenance
- Small stones may loosen
- Cost of additional stones

**Best for**: Centre stones needing protection (emerald in halo)

## Setting Recommendations by Gemstone

### Corundum (Ruby, Sapphire)

| Setting Type | Suitability | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Prong | Excellent | Any style works |
| Bezel | Excellent | Not necessary but works |
| Tension | Good | Suitable hardness |
| Any | Excellent | Most versatile gem |

### Emerald

| Setting Type | Suitability | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Bezel | Excellent | Best protection |
| Prong (6+) | Good | Protect corners |
| V-prong corners | Recommended | Protect vulnerable areas |
| Halo | Excellent | Adds protection |

**Special considerations**:
- Corners are vulnerable
- Inclusions weaken structure
- Avoid exposed edges

### Tanzanite

| Setting Type | Suitability | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Bezel | Best | Maximum protection |
| Protected prong | Good | Cover vulnerable areas |
| Halo | Good | Additional protection |
| Tension | Not recommended | Cleavage risk |

**Special considerations**:
- Perfect cleavage = fragile
- Avoid pressure points on cleavage planes
- Protective settings essential

### Opal

| Setting Type | Suitability | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Bezel | Best | Full protection |
| Cabochon bezel | Ideal | Standard for opals |
| Prong | Fair | Only if covered |
| Open back | Avoid | Unless boulder opal |

**Special considerations**:
- Very fragile
- Protect from impact
- Consider closed backs for some types
- Boulder opal more durable

### Pearl

| Setting Type | Suitability | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Post/cup | Standard | Pearl drilled, set on post |
| Partial bezel | Good | For half-drilled |
| Cage/basket | Good | For baroque |
| Prong | Rare | Can damage nacre |

**Special considerations**:
- Soft surface
- Avoid metal wear points
- Check post adhesive periodically

### Moonstone

| Setting Type | Suitability | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Bezel | Best | Protects cleavage |
| Cabochon bezel | Ideal | Standard style |
| Low prong | Fair | Vulnerable to chips |
| Open designs | Careful | Cleavage risk |

### Topaz

| Setting Type | Suitability | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Bezel | Good | Protects cleavage |
| Prong | Good | Cover base if possible |
| V-prong | Recommended | For pointed shapes |
| Tension | Fair | Cleavage concern |

**Special considerations**:
- Perfect basal cleavage
- Protect base of stone
- Avoid sharp impacts to base

### Kunzite

| Setting Type | Suitability | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Bezel | Best | Maximum protection |
| Protected prong | Fair | Cover vulnerable areas |
| Pendant setting | Good | Less impact risk |
| Ring setting | Careful | Only protective styles |

**Special considerations**:
- Perfect cleavage in two directions
- Light sensitivity
- Best for pendants/earrings

### Peridot

| Setting Type | Suitability | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Bezel | Good | Added protection |
| Prong | Good | Standard works |
| Tension | Fair | Moderate hardness |
| Channel | Good | Protected sides |

**Special considerations**:
- Sensitive to acids
- Moderate hardness (6.5-7)
- Reasonable durability

### Tourmaline

| Setting Type | Suitability | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Prong | Good | No cleavage |
| Bezel | Good | Not usually necessary |
| Any | Good | Reasonably durable |

**Special considerations**:
- No cleavage = good toughness
- Moderate hardness (7-7.5)
- Generally versatile

## Ring Shank Considerations

### Daily Wear Rings

| Feature | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| Shank width | Substantial (2mm+) |
| Shank thickness | Sturdy |
| Stone protection | Maximum |
| Low profile | Preferred |

### Occasional Wear Rings

| Feature | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| Shank width | Variable |
| Stone profile | Can be higher |
| Design | More flexible |

## Setting for Specific Uses

### Engagement Rings

| Priority | Recommendation |
|----------|----------------|
| Durability | Top priority |
| Stone security | Essential |
| Daily wear | Must withstand |
| Gemstone choice | Hardness 8+ ideal |

**Best gems**: Ruby, sapphire, spinel, chrysoberyl

### Men's Rings

| Feature | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| Setting | Flush, bezel, or heavy prong |
| Profile | Low |
| Stone | Very durable |
| Protection | Maximum |

### Earrings

| Feature | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| Stone durability | Less critical |
| Setting | Can be more delicate |
| Options | Wider range of gems |

### Pendants

| Feature | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| Stone durability | Less critical |
| Setting | Protective still good |
| Options | Wider range acceptable |
