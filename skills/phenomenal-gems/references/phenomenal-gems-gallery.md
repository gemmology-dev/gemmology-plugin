# Phenomenal Gems Gallery

Visual descriptions and identification criteria for optical phenomena in gemstones.

## Asterism (Star Effect)

### Six-Rayed Stars

| Gemstone | Cause | Quality Factors | Notable Sources |
|----------|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Star Ruby** | Rutile silk at 60° | Sharp rays, centred star, strong body colour | Burma, Sri Lanka, India |
| **Star Sapphire** | Rutile silk at 60° | Sharp intersecting rays, good translucency | Sri Lanka, Burma, Madagascar |
| **Star Garnet** | Rutile or hornblende | Rarer, usually almandine | Idaho (USA), India |
| **Star Rose Quartz** | Rutile silk | Usually pale, best in cabochons | Madagascar, Brazil |
| **Star Spinel** | Silk inclusions | Very rare | Sri Lanka, Burma |

**Ideal star characteristics**:
- Rays sharp and straight from edge to edge
- Star centred on dome apex
- Moves smoothly with light source
- All six rays equal length and brightness
- Good body colour beneath star

## Chatoyancy (Cat's Eye Effect)

### The "Milk and Honey" Effect
True cat's eye (chrysoberyl) shows:
- One side milky white when light from opposite side
- Other side honey-gold
- Flip sides when light source moves

### Cat's Eye Gemstones

| Gemstone | Cause | Quality Factors |
|----------|-------|-----------------|
| **Chrysoberyl Cat's Eye** | Hollow tubes or silk | Sharp eye, honey colour, strong milk-and-honey |
| **Alexandrite Cat's Eye** | Hollow tubes + Cr | Colour change + chatoyancy |
| **Tiger's Eye Quartz** | Fibrous crocidolite | Golden brown chatoyancy |
| **Hawk's Eye** | Unoxidised crocidolite | Blue-grey chatoyancy |
| **Cat's Eye Tourmaline** | Growth tubes | Various colours |
| **Cat's Eye Apatite** | Hollow tubes | Yellow-green common |
| **Cat's Eye Aquamarine** | Hollow tubes | Blue chatoyancy |
| **Cat's Eye Opal** | Silica structure | Play of colour + eye |
| **Sillimanite Cat's Eye** | Fibrous structure | Rare, strong effect |

**Ideal eye characteristics**:
- Razor-sharp line across dome
- Centred on dome apex
- Opens and closes smoothly
- Strong contrast with body colour
- No double or diffuse eye

## Adularescence

### Moonstone Varieties

| Type | Bodycolour | Adularescence | Source |
|------|------------|---------------|--------|
| **Blue Moonstone** | Colourless to white | Blue sheen | Sri Lanka (finest) |
| **Rainbow Moonstone** | Transparent to white | Multi-colour flash | India |
| **Grey Moonstone** | Grey | Blue sheen | India, Sri Lanka |
| **Peach Moonstone** | Peach/orange | White sheen | India, Tanzania |
| **Cat's Eye Moonstone** | Variable | Adularescence + eye | Sri Lanka |

**Cause**: Light scattering from thin albite lamellae within orthoclase feldspar

**Quality factors**:
- Intense blue (not white) sheen
- 3D "floating" appearance
- Transparency of body
- Sheen covers entire surface
- Even colour distribution

## Labradorescence

**Cause**: Light interference from repeated lamellar twinning

### Labradorite Quality

| Grade | Colours Displayed | Notes |
|-------|-------------------|-------|
| **Spectrolite** | Full spectrum | Finnish origin, highest grade |
| **Fine Labradorite** | Blue + green + gold | Strong play |
| **Standard** | Blue or green | Single colour flash |
| **Low** | Weak flashes | Poor contrast |

**Quality factors**:
- Range of colours (full spectrum most valuable)
- Intensity of play
- Coverage across stone
- Even colour when tilted
- Minimal dark areas

## Colour Change (Alexandrite Effect)

### Colour Change Gemstones

| Gemstone | Daylight | Incandescent | Change Strength |
|----------|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Alexandrite** | Green/blue-green | Red/purple-red | Strong to dramatic |
| **Colour-change Sapphire** | Blue/violet | Purple/red-purple | Variable |
| **Colour-change Garnet** | Blue-green/green | Red/purple | Can be dramatic |
| **Colour-change Diaspore** | Yellow-green | Pink/champagne | Moderate |
| **Colour-change Fluorite** | Blue | Purple | Subtle |
| **Colour-change Spinel** | Blue/grey | Violet/purple | Variable |

**Quality factors**:
- Strength of change (100% ideal)
- Attractiveness of both colours
- Saturation of colours
- Distinctness of change (not gradual)

### Grading Colour Change

| Grade | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **100%** | Complete change (e.g., pure green to pure red) |
| **80-99%** | Strong change, slight tint of other colour |
| **60-79%** | Moderate change, noticeable mix |
| **40-59%** | Weak change, colours blend |
| **<40%** | Minimal change, barely perceptible |

## Identification Tips

### Distinguishing Similar Phenomena

| Question | Asterism | Chatoyancy |
|----------|----------|------------|
| Number of rays? | 4, 6, or 12 | Single band |
| Movement with light? | Star moves | Eye opens/closes |
| Dome shape needed? | Yes | Yes |
| Inclusion orientation? | Multiple directions | Single direction |

| Question | Adularescence | Labradorescence |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Colour range? | Usually blue-white | Multi-colour possible |
| Light effect type? | Scattering (soft) | Interference (sharp) |
| Species? | Orthoclase feldspar | Plagioclase feldspar |
| Depth of effect? | Appears to float inside | Surface flash |

### Testing Notes

1. **Proper lighting essential** - Use single point light source
2. **Movement matters** - Rock stone gently to see phenomenon
3. **Background counts** - Dark backgrounds show effects better
4. **Magnification helps** - Loupe can reveal cause of phenomenon
5. **Compare to references** - Keep known examples for comparison
