# Gemstone Variety Guide

Distinguishing varieties within gemstone species.

## Corundum Varieties

### Ruby vs Pink Sapphire

| Factor | Ruby | Pink Sapphire |
|--------|------|---------------|
| Colour | Red | Pink |
| Saturation | Medium to high | Variable |
| Tone | Medium | Light to medium |
| Chromophore | High Cr³⁺ | Low Cr³⁺ |
| Fluorescence | Strong red | Moderate to strong |

**Boundary issue**: No universal standard; laboratories differ
- GIA: More inclusive for ruby
- Some labs: Stricter saturation requirements
- Market: Premium for "ruby" designation

### Padparadscha Criteria

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Colour | Pink-orange |
| Balance | Both colours present |
| Saturation | Light to medium |
| Origin | Sri Lanka traditionally (others now accepted) |
| Treatment | Heat accepted; diffusion not |

**Laboratory approaches**:
| Lab | Definition |
|-----|------------|
| GIA | Delicate pink-orange |
| Gübelin | Sri Lankan tradition plus others |
| SSEF | Balanced pink-orange |
| GRS | Various shades accepted |

## Beryl Varieties

### Emerald Definition

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Species | Beryl |
| Colour | Green |
| Chromophore | Cr and/or V |
| Saturation | Medium to high |

**Not emerald**: Green beryl coloured by iron only

### Grossular Varieties

| Variety | Colour | Chromophore | Trade Name |
|---------|--------|-------------|------------|
| Tsavorite | Green | V/Cr | - |

## Tourmaline Varieties

### Paraíba Definition

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Species | Elbaite tourmaline |
| Chromophore | Copper (Cu) |
| Colour | Blue to green (neon quality) |
| Manganese | Usually present (affects colour) |

**Trade/geographic considerations**:
- Brazil (Paraíba state): Original, highest premium
- Mozambique: "Paraíba-type" or "Cu-bearing"
- Nigeria: "Paraíba-type" or "Cu-bearing"

### Rubellite Definition

| Factor | Rubellite | Pink Tourmaline |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| Colour | Red to purplish-red | Pink |
| Saturation | Medium to high | Variable |
| Incandescent | Maintains red | May appear orange |

**Key test**: Rubellite should appear red in incandescent light

### Tourmaline Variety Names

## Spodumene Varieties

**Strict definition**: Chromium-coloured green spodumene from North Carolina
**Trade usage**: Often applied to any green spodumene

| Type | Colour Cause | Origin |
|------|--------------|--------|
| True hiddenite | Cr³⁺ | N. Carolina (rare) |
| Green spodumene | Fe | Various |
| Yellow-green | Mixed | Brazil, Afghanistan |

## Quartz Varieties

### Amethyst vs Violet Quartz

| Factor | Amethyst | Violet Quartz |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| Saturation | Medium to high | Weak |
| Value | Higher | Lower |
| Zoning | Angular | Variable |

### Citrine vs Heated Amethyst

| Factor | Natural Citrine | Heated Amethyst |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Colour | Yellow to orange | Orange-brown |
| Saturation | Usually moderate | Often strong |
| Colour distribution | Even | May show zoning |
| Availability | Rare | Common |
| Value | Higher | Lower |

### Prasiolite Origin

**Only natural source**: Montezuma, Brazil (extremely rare)
**Most market material**: Heated amethyst
