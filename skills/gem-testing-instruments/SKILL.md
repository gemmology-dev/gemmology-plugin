# Gem Testing Instruments

Guidance on using standard gemmological instruments for gemstone identification, following FGA (Gem-A) testing procedures.

## The Four Essential Instruments

### 1. Refractometer

**Purpose**: Measure refractive index (RI) and determine optical character.

**Components**:
- Glass prism (high RI, typically 1.81)
- Light source (sodium lamp preferred, LED acceptable)
- Scale (calibrated 1.30-1.81)
- Contact liquid (RI 1.81)

**Procedure**:
1. Clean gemstone table facet thoroughly
2. Apply small drop of contact liquid to prism
3. Place stone table-down on prism
4. View through eyepiece at arm's length
5. Read shadow edge position(s)
6. Rotate stone 180째 and re-read

**Reading Types**:

| Reading | Optical Character | Example |
|---------|-------------------|---------|
| Single constant edge | Isotropic (SR) | Spinel, Garnet |
| Two edges, both moving | Uniaxial (DR) | Corundum, Tourmaline |
| Two edges, one fixed, one moving | Biaxial (DR) | Topaz, Peridot |

**Limitations**:
- Maximum reading: 1.81 (RI of contact liquid)
- Stones over 1.81: Zircon, demantoid - use distant vision method
- Requires flat polished surface
- Curved surfaces give partial shadow

**Distant Vision Method** (for over-the-limit stones):
- Hold stone at arm's length above scale
- Look for vague shadow edge position

### 2. Polariscope

**Purpose**: Determine if stone is singly or doubly refractive, and detect strain.

**Components**:
- Two polarising filters (polariser and analyser)
- Light source
- Rotating platform

**Procedure**:
1. Cross polarisers (dark field)
2. Place stone on platform
3. Rotate stone 360째
4. Observe light/dark behaviour

**Interpreting Results**:

| Observation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| Stays dark through 360째 | Singly refractive (isotropic) |
| 4 light/dark alternations | Doubly refractive (anisotropic) |
| Stays light | Aggregate or ADR (anomalous) |
| Partial light/dark | Strain in isotropic material |

**ADR (Anomalous Double Refraction)**:
- Some isotropic stones show strain patterns
- Garnet: "Tabby extinction"
- Spinel: Stress birefringence
- Glass: Flow lines

**Conoscope Attachment**:
- Determines uniaxial vs biaxial
- Uniaxial: Maltese cross pattern
- Biaxial: Separate brushes

### 3. Dichroscope

**Purpose**: Observe pleochroism in coloured gemstones.

**Types**:
- **Calcite (Iceland spar)**: Shows two adjacent windows
- **Polarising**: Rotates to show each colour

**Procedure**:
1. View through strong light source
2. Observe two windows side by side
3. Rotate stone 90째
4. Note colour differences

**Interpreting Results**:

| Observation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| Two identical colours | Isotropic (no pleochroism) |
| Two different colours | Doubly refractive |
| Strong colour difference | Strong pleochroism |

**Diagnostic Applications**:

| Stones | Dichroism | Use |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| Ruby vs Red Spinel | Ruby: purplish/orangey-red; Spinel: none | Separation |
| Sapphire | Violetish-blue/Greenish-blue | Confirmation |
| Tanzanite | Very strong trichroism | Diagnostic |
| Iolite | Blue/grey/yellowish | Diagnostic |

### 4. Spectroscope

**Purpose**: View absorption spectrum to identify colour-causing elements.

**Types**:
- **Diffraction grating**: Linear spectrum, portable
- **Prism**: Elongated red end, higher resolution

**Procedure**:
1. Illuminate stone with strong white light
2. Position stone between light and spectroscope
3. Focus on spectrum
4. Note positions of dark absorption lines/bands

**Reading the Spectrum**:
- Scale in nanometres (nm)
- Red end: 700nm
- Violet end: 400nm
- Record positions of absorption bands

Reference: See `optical-properties` skill for diagnostic spectra.

## Secondary Instruments

### Chelsea Colour Filter

**Purpose**: Quick colour screening for certain gems.

**Mechanism**: Transmits only deep red (~690nm) and yellow-green (~570nm).

**Reactions**:

| Stone | Reaction | Note |
|-------|----------|------|
| Colombian Emerald | Pinkish-red | Chromium content |
| Synthetic Emerald | Strong red | Higher Cr |
| Zambian Emerald | Weak/greenish | Lower Cr, higher Fe |
| Aquamarine | Green | No chromium |
| Blue Cobalt Glass | Red | Cobalt absorption |
| Blue Synthetic Spinel | Red | Cobalt content |
| Natural Blue Spinel | Green | No cobalt |

**Limitations**:
- Not diagnostic alone
- Some natural emeralds stay green
- Some synthetics don't react strongly

### Ultraviolet Lamp

**Purpose**: Observe fluorescence reactions.

**Types**:
- **Long-wave (LW)**: 365nm
- **Short-wave (SW)**: 254nm

**Safety**: Avoid direct eye exposure, especially SW.

**Procedure**:
1. Dark room essential
2. Test both LW and SW
3. Note colour and intensity
4. Check phosphorescence (afterglow)

**Diagnostic Reactions**:

See `optical-properties` skill for detailed fluorescence guide.

### Microscope

**Purpose**: Examine inclusions, surface features, growth patterns.

**Specifications**:
- 10x to 60x magnification
- Darkfield illumination essential
- Brightfield, oblique, polarised options

**Lighting Techniques**:

| Method | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| Darkfield | Most inclusions, general observation |
| Brightfield | Colour zoning, transparency |
| Oblique/fibre optic | Silk, surface features |
| Polarised | Strain, twinning, ADR |
| Immersion | Clarity enhancement |

### Hydrostatic Balance

**Purpose**: Measure specific gravity accurately.

**Procedure**:
1. Weigh stone in air (W_air)
2. Weigh stone suspended in water (W_water)
3. Calculate: SG = W_air / (W_air - W_water)

**Accuracy Requirements**:
- Balance accurate to 0.01ct minimum
- Distilled water at room temperature
- Stone must be free of surface water

### Thermal Conductivity Tester

**Purpose**: Distinguish certain materials (primarily diamond testing).

**For Coloured Stones**:
- Limited usefulness
- Can distinguish some synthetics
- Moissanite tester: distinguishes moissanite from diamond

### Heavy Liquids

**Purpose**: Quick SG estimation by flotation.

**Common Liquids**:
| Liquid | SG | Floats | Sinks |
|--------|-----|--------|-------|
| Methylene Iodide | 3.32 | Quartz, Beryl | Corundum, Garnet |
| Bromoform | 2.89 | Opal | Most gems |

**Safety**: Many heavy liquids are toxic. Hydrostatic method preferred.

## Testing Sequence

### Recommended Order

1. **Visual examination** (loupe, microscope)
   - Body colour, lustre, clarity
   - Visible inclusions

2. **Refractive index** (refractometer)
   - RI value(s)
   - Birefringence
   - Optical sign if determinable

3. **Optical character** (polariscope)
   - Singly or doubly refractive
   - Strain/ADR

4. **Pleochroism** (dichroscope)
   - Colours observed
   - Strength

5. **Spectrum** (spectroscope)
   - Absorption lines/bands

6. **Fluorescence** (UV lamp)
   - LW and SW reactions

7. **Specific gravity** (if needed)
   - Hydrostatic method

8. **Advanced testing** (if needed)
   - Microscopic inclusion study
   - Advanced spectroscopy

## Common Test Combinations

### Separating Similar Stones

**Ruby vs Red Spinel**:
| Test | Ruby | Red Spinel |
|------|------|------------|
| RI | 1.762-1.770 | 1.712-1.736 |
| Polariscope | DR (4 blinks) | SR (stays dark) |
| Dichroscope | 2 colours | 1 colour |
| Spectrum | Chromium doublet | Organ pipe |

**Blue Sapphire vs Blue Spinel**:
| Test | Sapphire | Spinel |
|------|----------|--------|
| RI | 1.762-1.770 | 1.712-1.736 |
| Polariscope | DR | SR |
| Dichroscope | 2 blues | 1 blue |
| Spectrum | Iron bands 450-470nm | Cobalt or none |

**Natural vs Synthetic Ruby**:
| Test | Natural | Flame-fusion |
|------|---------|--------------|
| Microscope | Silk, fingerprints | Curved striae, bubbles |
| UV (LW) | Strong red | Very strong red |
| Inclusions | Mineral crystals | Gas bubbles |
