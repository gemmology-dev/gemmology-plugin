# Spectroscope Patterns Guide

Diagnostic absorption spectra for gemstone identification.

## Using the Spectroscope

### Light Sources

| Source | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|--------|------------|---------------|
| Incandescent | Continuous spectrum | Heat, brightness |
| Fibre-optic | Concentrated, cool | May be weaker |
| Daylight | Natural | Variable, scattered |
| LED (warm) | Cool, even | May lack red end |

### Viewing Techniques

**Transmission** (transparent stones):
- Light through stone to eye
- Best for most gems

**Reflection** (opaque/cabochons):
- Light reflects off surface
- Less detail usually

**Immersion**:
- Stone in liquid
- Reduces surface reflections

## Corundum Spectra

### Ruby (Chromium)

| Feature | Position (nm) | Intensity |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| Emission doublet | 694.2, 692.8 | Bright lines (not absorption) |
| Absorption line | 668 | Weak |
| Absorption line | 659 | Weak |
| Broad band | 550 (centre) | Strong |
| Lines | 476, 475, 468 | Weak |

**Key diagnostic**: Bright 694/693nm doublet (fluorescence lines)

**Iron-rich rubies** (Thai): 694nm lines appear as dark absorption

### Blue Sapphire (Iron/Titanium)

| Feature | Position (nm) | Intensity |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| Band | 471 | Strong |
| Band | 460 | Strong |
| Band | 450 | Very strong |

**Key diagnostic**: Triple band at 450-471nm; 450nm strongest

**By origin**:
- Kashmir: Very weak spectrum
- Burma: Moderate spectrum
- Australian/Thai: Strong 450nm band

### Yellow/Orange Sapphire

| Feature | Position (nm) |
|---------|---------------|
| Band | 450 | Iron absorption |
| May show | 471, 460 | Weaker than blue |

### Padparadscha

Shows weak chromium lines + iron bands

## Emerald Spectra

### Chromium-Coloured (Most Natural)

| Feature | Position (nm) | Intensity |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| Doublet | 683, 680 | Strong (diagnostic) |
| Line | 662 | Moderate |
| Line | 646 | Moderate |
| Broad band | 600-630 | Partial absorption |
| Cutoff | Below 470 | Violet absorbed |

**Key diagnostic**: 683/680nm doublet confirms chromium

### Vanadium-Dominant

| Feature | Position (nm) |
|---------|---------------|
| Broad band | 600 region | Without sharp chromium lines |

May lack distinct doublet; more diffuse absorption

## Recording Spectra

### Standard Notation

```
ABSORPTION SPECTRUM:
Strong bands: 505, 527, 576nm
Moderate bands: 462, 438nm
Weak bands: 428, 404nm
Pattern: Consistent with almandine garnet
```

### Sketch Method

Draw scale 400-700nm, mark:
- Strong features: thick lines
- Moderate: medium lines
- Weak: thin lines
- Cutoffs: shaded areas
