# Refractometer Readings Guide

Interpreting and troubleshooting refractometer measurements.

## Understanding the Reading

### The Shadow Edge

| Observation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|
| Single sharp edge | Isotropic material (SR) |
| Two edges, same position | Reading along optic axis |
| Two separate edges | Birefringent material (DR) |
| Blurry/indistinct edge | RI near liquid limit or problem |

### Reading Scale

- Scale typically reads 1.30 to 1.81
- Standard RI liquid: 1.79-1.81
- Read to nearest 0.001
- Higher edge = higher RI

## Birefringent Stone Readings

### Taking Accurate Readings

1. Place stone on refractometer
2. Note initial shadow edge position(s)
3. Rotate stone 180° while watching
4. Record highest and lowest readings
5. Calculate: BR = High RI - Low RI

### Interpreting Movement

| Movement Pattern | Indicates |
|------------------|-----------|
| Both edges stationary | Reading on optic axis |
| Both edges move together | Not on optic axis |
| Edges move apart/together | Normal birefringent |
| Edges "flip" positions | Passed through optic axis |

### Maximum Birefringence

**Important**: You only get true maximum birefringence when:
- Stone oriented perpendicular to optic axis
- Taking readings at right angle to axis

Actual readings often show less than maximum BR.

## Optic Sign Determination

### Method

1. Find the higher and lower RI readings
2. Rotate stone and observe which moves
3. Fixed reading = principal ray
4. Moving reading = varying ray

### Interpretation

| If Fixed Reading Is: | And It's The: | Sign Is: |
|----------------------|---------------|----------|
| Fixed | Higher of two | Positive (+) |
| Fixed | Lower of two | Negative (-) |

### Uniaxial vs Biaxial

| Uniaxial | Biaxial |
|----------|---------|
| One fixed, one moving | Both may move |
| Fixed = ω (ordinary) | Three principal indices |
| Moving = ε (extraordinary) | α, β, γ |

## Common Reading Scenarios

### Single Isotropic Reading

**Causes**:
- Truly isotropic (cubic, glass, opal)
- Reading down optic axis of uniaxial
- Very low birefringence

**What to do**:
- Confirm with polariscope
- If DR in polariscope, reorient stone

### Over-the-Limit (OTL)

**Symptoms**:
- No shadow edge visible
- Stone appears to "float" above scale

**Common OTL materials**:
| Material | RI |
|----------|-----|
| Zircon | 1.81-1.98 |
| Diamond | 2.42 |
| Moissanite | 2.65-2.69 |
| CZ | 2.15-2.18 |
| Strontium titanate | 2.41 |
| Rutile | 2.62-2.90 |
| Sphene | 1.84-2.03 |
| Demantoid | 1.89 |
| Sphalerite | 2.37 |
| Cassiterite | 1.99-2.09 |

**Alternative tests for OTL**:
- Specific gravity
- Spectrum
- Thermal conductivity (diamond testers)
- Microscopy

### Spot Readings (Cabochons)

**Technique**:
1. Apply small drop of RI liquid
2. Touch curved surface to glass
3. Look for bright spot on scale

**Accuracy**: ±0.01 (less precise than flat facet)

### Distant Vision Method

For very curved surfaces:
1. Position stone normally
2. Back away from eyepiece
3. Look for general shadow area
4. Estimate RI range

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: No Reading Visible

| Cause | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Stone not touching glass | Reposition |
| Too little RI liquid | Add more |
| RI above liquid limit | Use higher index liquid or alternate test |
| Dirty glass or stone | Clean thoroughly |
| Stone too small | Use tweezers, steady positioning |

### Problem: Blurry Shadow Edge

| Cause | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Stone RI near liquid RI | Look carefully for faint edge |
| Dirt on glass | Clean |
| Stone surface scratched | Use different facet |
| Very high dispersion | Look for rainbow edge |

### Problem: Multiple Shadow Edges

| Cause | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Birefringent (normal) | Record both, calculate BR |
| Doublet | Examine microscopically |
| Coated stone | Look for coating signs |
| Two minerals present | Examine microscopically |

### Problem: Edge Moves During Reading

| Cause | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Stone rotating | Hold steady |
| RI liquid moving | Refresh liquid |
| Birefringent (normal) | Take readings at multiple positions |

## Special Cases

### Metamict Zircon

| Type | RI | BR |
|------|-----|-----|
| High (normal) | 1.92-1.98 | 0.059 |
| Intermediate | 1.85-1.93 | Variable |
| Low (metamict) | 1.78-1.85 | Near zero |

**Note**: Radiation damage reduces crystallinity and BR

### ADR (Anomalous Double Refraction)

Some isotropic materials show slight DR due to strain:
- Synthetic spinel (common)
- Diamond (can occur)
- Garnet (rare)

**Detection**: Patchy birefringence in polariscope

### Aggregates

Polycrystalline materials may show:
- Spot readings only
- Variable readings
- Single average reading

Examples: Jadeite, nephrite, chalcedony

## Recording Readings

### Standard Format

```
RI: 1.762-1.770
BR: 0.008
Optic sign: Negative (-)
Character: Uniaxial
```

### Detailed Format

```
RI higher: 1.770 (extraordinary)
RI lower: 1.762 (ordinary)
BR observed: 0.008
BR expected: 0.008
Optic sign: Negative
Character: Uniaxial
Conclusion: Consistent with corundum
```
